Obamaâ€™s 2013 Budget Proposal Critique

Source: www.nytimes.com/interactive/2012/02/13/us/politics/2013-budget-proposal-graphic.html

Domain
  • General populace who want a clear way to view the proposed spendings for departments in the US government.
Data / Task Abstraction
  • Forecasted spending amount
  • Budget % increase/decrease
  • Spending types
  • Total amount for each department
Idiom
  • Uses a circular packed bubble chart to represent the spending of each department.

  • A color scale is used to represented the proposed budget % increase/decrease from the previous year.

  • Types of Spending tab splits the chart into the two spending types.

  • Changes tab showcases a graph of discretionary spending % increase/decrease (y-axis) for each department (x-axis).

  • Department Totals tab splits bubbles into clusters that sum the total spending per department.
Critiques
  • Bubbles too small: Need to hover over bubbles to know what they are, but some of them are too small and packed together which can make hovering difficult.

  • Lost ordering: Trying to examine bubbles under Department Totals tab is still difficult, as bubbles are mashed together without the highest to lowest change % ordering that's present in other tabs.

  • Unclear x-axis: Data in Changes tab is sorted by departments on the x-axis, but is not indicated.

  • Limited comparism: While users are able to compare spending between departments in the Changes tab, it is only for discetionary spending.

  • Overlapping bubbles: Bubbles in the Changes tab tend to overlap, making it even harder to select smaller values that are clustered together.

  • Redundant colour scale: Increase/decrease % is denoted on the y-axis in the changes tab, doing the same thing as the green to red colour scale.


Proposed Improvement


Improvements
  • Department changes tab: Create a new tab that focuses on the spending changes and amount for each department.

  • Clearer comparism: The addition of spending on the x-axis in conjuction with increase/decrease % allows users to better compare the spending areas of each department in an organized fashion.

  • Logarithmic scale: The spending scale is ogarithmic to ensure that smaller bubbles are spread further apart, highlighting their existence and reducing overlap possibility.

  • Dropdown menu: Users have an option to select which departments they want to examine, rather than having to rely on the unorganized Department Totals tab.

  • More options for colour: Allows users to swap the colour scale to a different category, making visual identification faster.


UK Income Budget in 2015

Source: informationisbeautiful.net/visualizations/uk-government-spending-incomes-outcomes/

Domain
  • General populace who want a clear way to view where taxes come from and how they are used by the UK government.
Data / Task Abstraction
  • Tax income amount by total and category
  • Tax spending amount by category
  • % of income and spending by category
  • Spending amount contributed per day by taxpayers
Idiom
  • Uses a tree diagram - top tree shows income and bottom tree shows spendings.

  • Branches out to nodes based on money received/spent, where larger sum of money have larger nodes and smaller sum of money have smaller nodes.
Critiques
  • Information overload: Too many different formats of data being presented at once creates a visual salad.

  • Inconsistent summation: Total income is shown, but not total outgoing budget so you can't compare both. Likely due to design or data constraints.

  • Unclear parent-child relations: Design can be unclear in signifying that nodes along branches are part of the total. Also has some visual oddities.

  • Colour confusion: Categories with the same colour seems to be imply somethind that is not actually there.


Proposed Improvement


Improvements
  • Force diagram: Reformatted to a force diagram that users can intercact with and is able to fit both categories into the same area.

  • Just enough info shown: Nodes can show just the name and amount on them to ensure visual clarity. Further data can be hidden behind tooltips.

  • Links and arrows: Denotes the parent child relationship between nodes and highlights the existence of smaller nodes.

  • Dynamic node sizes: Gives users quick visual feedback on which areas are worth more.

  • Unique colouring: Helps to differentiate categories better.
