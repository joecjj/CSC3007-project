Jia Jun, Yi Kang, Stuart



Singapore's 2021 Fiscal Position

Source: https://data.gov.sg/dataset/government-fiscal-position

Domain
  • General populace who want a clear way to view the proposed spendings for departments in the US government.
Data / Task Abstraction
  • Forecasted spending amount
  • Budget % increase/decrease
  • Spending types
  • Total amount for each department
Idiom
  • Uses a circular packed bubble chart to represent the spending of each department.

  • A color scale is used to represented the proposed budget % increase/decrease from the previous year.

  • Types of Spending tab splits the chart into the two spending types.

  • Changes tab showcases a graph of discretionary spending % increase/decrease (y-axis) for each department (x-axis).

  • Department Totals tab splits bubbles into clusters that sum the total spending per department.
Critiques
  • Bubbles too small: Need to hover over bubbles to know what they are, but some of them are too small and packed together which can make hovering difficult.

  • Lost ordering: Trying to examine bubbles under Department Totals tab is still difficult, as bubbles are mashed together without the highest to lowest change % ordering that's present in other tabs.

  • Unclear x-axis: Data in Changes tab is sorted by departments on the x-axis, but is not indicated.

  • Limited comparism: While users are able to compare spending between departments in the Changes tab, it is only for discetionary spending.

  • Overlapping bubbles: Bubbles in the Changes tab tend to overlap, making it even harder to select smaller values that are clustered together.

  • Redundant colour scale: Increase/decrease % is denoted on the y-axis in the changes tab, doing the same thing as the green to red colour scale.
