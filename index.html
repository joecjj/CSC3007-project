What caused Singapore's budget to increase in 2020?

Source: https://data.gov.sg/dataset/government-fiscal-position

Why are we doing this?
  • We noticed an interesting anomaly in Singapore's budget data.
  • We wanted to make a storytelling-focused visualization that informs viewers of this anomaly.
  • Aimed to be accessible to the public.
What data are we using?
  • This is Singapore's fiscal position data, showing monetary values for revenue and expenditure.
  • Taken from data.gov and cleaned using spreadsheets.
How are we telling the story?
  • A series of charts, each serving as a chapter in the story.
  • Mimics the way we discovered this anomaly.
Why the bar chart?
  • Visually discernible way to spot trends in the government's budget through the years.
What does this bar chart tell?
  • In 2020, the annual amount in the government's budget saw a monumental increase.
What does this bar chart not tell?
  • What happened to the government's budget in 2020.

Diving into 2020


Why the packed bubble chart?
  • Group items in the 2020 government's budget by categories
  • Easy to compare different categories with each other.
  • Side-by-side comparism visualizes the ratio of how much money is received and spent.
What does this packed bubble chart tell?
  • Expenditure was much higher than revenue in 2020.
What does this packed bubble chart not tell?
  • The change in an item's value over the years.
